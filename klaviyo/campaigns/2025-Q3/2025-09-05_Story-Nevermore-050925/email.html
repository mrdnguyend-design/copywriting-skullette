Stranger (in my inbox):
“Your store must be selling poorly, huh? No customers? Too much time to write all these long stories…”

Me (holding a cup of black coffee, blinking slowly):
…Excuse me?

I didn’t reply.
Let her think that.

But two days later—when I didn’t send anything?

DING.
A text.
“Are you busy these days? You okay?”

😂 So now I’m too quiet?

Too much time.
Too little time.
Apparently, I live in a Schrödinger’s schedule.

But honestly…
It made me wonder:

How many of you are like her? Watching? Waiting? Quietly judging… but still reading?

The truth?

I’m just an introverted woman trying to sell skull bags...
without showing my face.

And email?
That’s how I talk to you.

Not “customers.”
Soulmates.

Yes, I run a business.
Yes, I’m busy.

But sometimes?

I’m free.

And that night?
I shut the laptop.
Lit a candle.
Opened The Raven.

And I swear… something in my chest cracked open.

The bird.
The stare.
The tapping.
The word:

“Nevermore.”

It whispered like a curse that refused to fade.

So I did what any skull-obsessed, slightly unhinged woman would do.

I sketched.
From “free time” to “possessed” in 0.2 seconds.

A raven.
Perched on a skull.
Red burning behind it.
Black leather carrying it.
Sharp.
Deadly.
Cool.

And now…

Here it is.

Now before you scream “TAKE MY MONEY”…

Bad news.

The leather hasn’t even arrived yet.
(Nearly peak season. My supplier? Slower than a séance.)

So no, you can’t buy this Raven bag.
Not yet.

This email?

It’s just me showing off.
A little pride.
A little madness.
A little… warning.

Because while you wait for this?

The bags that DO exist are quietly walking off into the shadows.

And here’s the twist?
Each one is going out with a FREE matching wallet.

Yep.
Because I’m “too generous” in my “free time.” 🙃

So if the Raven’s whispering “nevermore” in your ear about waiting…

Listen to her.

Because once these bags are gone?

That’s exactly what you’ll hear.
