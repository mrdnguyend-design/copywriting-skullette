Last night…

I had the most dramatic dream of my life.

I was in a black velvet dress.
Candlelight flickering everywhere.
The scent of old paper and expensive leather in the air.

And a long line of women — dressed in black lace, fishnets, Doc Martens — all waiting outside a dark little boutique.

MY boutique.

The Skullette store.
A real, physical, brick-and-mortar Skullette store.
And I was standing behind the register (okay, it was a coffin) sending out black shopping bags like spells.

Everything was perfect.

So perfect…

That when I woke up this morning, I LAUGHED out loud like some Victorian widow who’d just discovered how to use TikTok.

Because the whole thing felt so real.

And I thought:
“God, I really do dream big, huh?”

But THEN.

The universe — ever the dramatic queen — said:
“Oh, she thinks she’s done? LET’S SEND A SIGN.”

And this landed in my inbox:

“Dude!!
 I just noticed that you are in Albuquerque! I grew up in the NE Heights! Graduated from Manzano in '84!
 My sister is the skull devotee — I sent her your info and just told her you're in town!
 Maybe next time I'm home we can cruise up that way and hunt you down!
 Do you have a store as well? This is too cool!
 The person who sent you that backhanded compliment is sort of dumb.
 I know from experience that a cool bag is a show-stopper!
 I want to score one of yours for my collection.
 Thanks again for your great emails.”

Uh.

Ma’am.

Are you… planning to hunt me down?

Because I put an address in the footer of my email?

And do you think when you arrive at said address, you’ll find a magical gothic wonderland with skull chandeliers and a gothic saleslady whispering, “Welcome… to the shadows…”?

NO.

You will find:
– A stressed woman with a tape gun in one hand and a half-eaten protein bar in the other
– A warehouse floor that has personally tripped me at least 7 times
– And if you’re lucky… maybe Dylan the warehouse guy complaining about USPS while dodging falling boxes

But I get it.

Your email reminded me how powerful this little dream of mine is.
Because even though I don’t have a storefront yet...

I do have something even better.

I have YOU.
And the thought that one day, we’ll both stand inside a real Skullette store and laugh about how this all started with a tiny black bag in a brown box.

So tell me...

Would you want a physical Skullette store one day?

Or do you prefer your bags to appear mysteriously at your doorstep… with zero risk of tripping over Julie in the backroom?

Just hit reply and tell me.
(And if you're the “hunt-you-down” lady… I will send you GPS directions to the nearest Hot Topic instead. 💀)
