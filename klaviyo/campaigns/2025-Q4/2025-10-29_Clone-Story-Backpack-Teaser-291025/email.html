“Royalties.”
That’s what she said.

I was making coffee.
She marched in.

Purple hoodie. Wild curls. Clutching her Lunacat bag like it was Exhibit A.

Drops into my office chair.
Swivels.
Points.

“I want this on a backpack.”

I blink.
She does not.

“For school. You know. Where kids actually need backpacks?”

Right. Fourth grade.
Fair point.

I tried to brush it off.
(You ever try to brush off a 9-year-old mid-monologue?)
Yeah, it didn’t work.

She went full pitch mode:

  • Mini bags are cute, but bad for posture.
  • Two straps > one.
  • She can’t fit lunch, books, AND fourth-grade gossip.
  • Everyone in her class wants Lunacat.
  • And — this is where I choked on my coffee —
    “If you use my idea, I want royalties.”

I said:
“ROYALTIES?!”

She shrugged.
“That’s how business works.”

(I hate how much I respect her.)

Anyway…
I caved.

Spent the weekend stitching leather, adjusting straps, whispering dark prayers over the zipper.

And when it was done?
It looked incredible.

So now I’m wondering:

What if we made backpacks?
Not just for her.
But for you.

For every rebel who’s ever messaged me:

  • “Do you make anything with two straps?”
  • “I love Skullette but totes kill my shoulders.”
  • “I need something for everyday chaos.”

Maybe… this is it.

Same bold designs.
Same gothic soul.
Just… easier on your spine.

Would you want one?
