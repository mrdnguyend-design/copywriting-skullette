Quick update before we go any further:

Over 250 witches have already registered.

This morning, I’m still riding that tiny dopamine buzz when Alice from Operations walks up to me with her clipboard.

Not running. Not panicking. Just… calm.

Which is how I know something is very wrong.

“Julie,” she says, not even looking up.

“We have a math problem.”

(Oh no.)

She points at the first section of the warehouse.

“Lucky bags for Friday. 60. Good.”

Then she shifts the clipboard slightly and taps another section.

“Guarantee-win bags. 200.”

Pause. “Not good.”

I blink. “Why not?”

She looks at me like I’ve just asked why fire burns.

“Because you promised everyone at least 50% OFF.”

“And you have more than 250 people.”

“That’s… math.”

I stare at the warehouse.

Rows of leather.

All of it suddenly looking very… finite.

And then I do what any reasonable business owner does when cornered by basic arithmetic:

I double down. “Add the backpacks… the totes. Hmm… ALL OF THEM.”

You can almost hear the calculator in her head clicking away.

Finally, she nods. “I’ll get the team.”

And now?

Forklifts beeping. Boxes dragged. Shelves rearranged.

Alice is back at her clipboard like nothing ever happened.

All that chaos?
