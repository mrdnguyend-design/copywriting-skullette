Last Tuesday, I open my inbox and see this:

"Hey Julie, love my Nyxrose bag but have you ever thought about making charms? Would be perfect for adding personality. Just a thought! - Lexi"

Cute idea, I think.

Then Wednesday:

"I bought three of your bags and I keep thinking... they'd look AMAZING with some kind of gothic charm. Skulls, moons, something dark. Are you planning anything like that? - Morgan"

Okay... interesting.

Then Thursday I get this one:

"PLEASE tell me you're making bag charms. I've been searching everywhere for ones that match the Skullette vibe and everything out there is either cheap Hot Topic garbage or boring Pandora-style stuff. HELP. - Ash"

I screenshot all three and text them to Jade:

Me: "Are people losing their minds about charms or is it just my inbox?"

Jade: "Check our DMs. I've gotten like 10 messages about it this week."

Me: "...what?"

Jade: "Yeah. Apparently everyone wants dangly things now."

So I did what any sane person would do.

I ignored it.

(Kidding.)

I actually started researching charms. And here's what I found:

The goth/alt charm market is either cheap mall-goth plastic skulls... or $200 luxury brand logos that have nothing to do with our aesthetic.

There's nothing in between.

Nothing that's ACTUALLY gothic. Actually quality. Actually Skullette.

So Jade and I spent the last week designing a collection.

Real metal. Real leather. Real hardware.

Stuff that looks like it belongs on a witch's altar, not a teenager's backpack.

But here's where it gets interesting:

Yesterday I showed the designs to my friend Sara (who carries a cream-colored Coach bag and drinks pumpkin spice lattes in July).

Sara: "Charms? Aren't those kind of... childish?"

Me: "..."

Sara: "I mean, they're cute for like, teenagers, but for a BUSINESS? Seems gimmicky."

I smiled.

"Perfect," I said. "Then they're not for you."

Because here's the thing—

If Basic Sara thinks charms are "too much," that means we're on the right track.

The women who carry Skullette bags don't accessorize to blend in.

They accessorize to make people uncomfortable.

And THAT is exactly what these charms are designed to do.
