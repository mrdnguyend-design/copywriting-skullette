I asked for charm ideas yesterday.

Ms. Sharon sent me a photo.

No explanation. No “hope you like it.” Just this:

“So it's not a charm... but maybe charming? Bad joke. I don't apologize.”

Then I opened the attachment.

And just… stopped.

(Image: bat chalkboard drawing)

Really? She DREW this.

In her kitchen. On a chalkboard. With her own hands.

Not an AI prompt. Not some Pinterest save. Not a “wouldn’t this be cool” screenshot.

Actual chalk. Actual wings. Actual Dracula quote at the bottom.

The bat isn’t cute. It’s not “gothic chic.” It’s WATCHING you.

Like it already knows you’re going to buy the bag it ends up on.

(Sharon’s exact words: “I have 8 Skullettes now. Somehow you have triggered that in my DNA after 57 years. Spellbound, perhaps?”)

Yeah, Sharon. That’s the goal.

Here’s what I love about this.

While every other brand is out there hiring “brand consultants” and running focus groups and asking their beige customers what shade of taupe they prefer…

Sharon just grabbed some chalk and drew what SHOULD exist.

No committee. No approval process. No “let’s make it more mainstream.”

Just pure witchy audacity.

(This is exactly why I don’t design bags for Basic Brendas. They’d never grab chalk in the first place. They’re too busy asking their husbands if black is “too much.”)

So here’s the deal.

I can’t promise this exact bat will become a bag. Design timelines are long. Leather suppliers are slow. And I’m already designing three other things that are fighting for brain space.
