## Body
I’ve matched a lot of Skullette bags to outfits before.

The black-on-black leather look? Easy.

Bloodrose? Pair her with velvet.

La Roja? Red lips. Done.

But this design -Catrinelle?

She... complicated things...

First time I held her, I stood in front of the mirror for an hour thinking:

“What exactly do you wear with a baroque sugar skull bathed in purple and gemstones?”

Too much black—and she glares at you.

Too much color—and she mocks you.

She doesn’t want to be part of your outfit.

She wants to dominate it.

And honestly? She deserves to.

Her design pulls fromDía de los Muertos, Baroque glam, maximalist fashion, and gothic romance—she’s not just a bag.

She’s an altar. A celebration. A little too much on purpose.

But that means styling her isn’t easy.

It’s a challenge.

You’ll second guess.

You’ll overthink.

You’ll have to leave half your closet behind.

Because she doesn’t walk with basics.

She needs confidence. She demands intention.

And maybe that’s not for you.

If you want a bag that’s easy to style…

If you prefer things that go with “everything”…

If you need your accessories to shut up and cooperate…

She’s not your girl.

Catrinelle doesn’t match outfits. She devours them.

She isn’t elegant in the way you’re used to. She’s sacred. She’s rebellious.

And if that scares you a little?

Good.

Come meet Catrinelle anyway.

She’s not looking for someone perfect. Just someone brave enough to try.

Stay wicked,

JulieFounder, Skullette
