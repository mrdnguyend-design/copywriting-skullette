My room was dark.My coffee? Colder than my soul.And my brain? An empty cave echoing with one desperate thought:

"I NEED a new idea."

Then it happened.

I blurted, half-conscious, to my daughter:“Leave me alone—I’m trying to think.”

She blinked. Tilted her head.Then in that terrifying way only children can do, she lit up with a plan.

“Okay,” she said, “but you should make something aboutLunacat.”

Luna… what?
