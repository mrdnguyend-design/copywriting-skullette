I'm standing in my kitchen, black coffee in hand, contemplating whether three espressos before 9 AM is"self-care"or"a cry for help."

GLORIA:"Julie!! You HAVE to read this review!! It's the sweetest thing ever!! 💕"

ME:"Is someone mad at us?"

GLORIA:"No!! The opposite!!"

ME:"Did we accidentally send someone two orders?"

GLORIA:"No!! Just READ IT!!"

I open Trustpilot, fully expecting a nice but unremarkable review.

It's from Maria. Five stars.

"I ended up getting the Noxaria small satchel and wallet. After seeing it a lot of times, it just kept popping up everywhere I went on the internet. So I knew it was meant for me, kind of like destiny."

I read it twice.

ME:"This IS sweet."

GLORIA:"RIGHT?! She said it was DESTINY!! Like the bag literally found her!!"

GLORIA:"That sounds creepy when you say it like that 😟"

ME:"It's not creepy. It's just... advertising."

GLORIA:"But what if it's BOTH? What if Facebook is just helping destiny happen??"

I stare at my phone.
