## Body
Do you remember that email I sent?

The one about a bat design for Skullette?

I thought it was just a passing whisper.

A little chaos I scribbled in my sketchbook at 2AM.

Something I’d revisit next year.

Then my inboxexploded like a blood moon.

Most of the replies were exactly what I hoped for:

Dark. Sharp. Gothic.

Bat wings with attitude.

Moons that don’t shine —they glare.

But then...

One reply caught me off guard —in the best way possible.

It came from a fantasy artist named Donna Antonucci:

“Fox-faced bat 🦇 would be so cute — I wouldn’t want a scary bat.”

I paused.

Cute?!

My brain short-circuited.

Skullette doesn’t do cute.

It’s skulls.Shadows.

Leather that looks like it could survive an exorcism.

Our version of“sweet”is a dagger wrapped in velvet.

But I couldn’t stop thinking about her idea.

It lingered.

In that strange, beautiful way only a really good story does.

And then I saw it.

A fantasy-style bat.

Not scary. Not soft. Just... ethereal.

Roses and moons. Purple haze.

Glossy wings that glint like secrets.

Should I do it? A fantasy bat? A whole collection?

Maybe some of you secretly love that softer magic — you just haven’t told me yet.

Maybe some of you love this dreamy style too…

And just haven’t said it yet.

But then I sat back.

Because Skullette has always had a clear direction.

It doesn’t follow trends.

It doesn’t do soft.

It shows up with bold design, unapologetic edge, and leather that bites.

So… no.

We’re not doing cute.(Not yet.)

But maybe — just maybe —

One day, a fantasy version will slip into the shadows.

A secret drop.

(If I can ever make one that still feels Skullette… but that might take a long time)

For now—If you're here for the original Skullette slay...

You're going to want to see this.

This is the deal my accountant made me promise not to repeat.

It still makes Finance sigh every time someone uses it.
