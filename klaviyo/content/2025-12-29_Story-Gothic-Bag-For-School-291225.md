## Body
Hey ,

“Do you think Skullette bags are suitable for school?”

Honestly? I think yes.(actually, just some of them absolutely are)

And before you ask,“Julie… why do you suddenly have this idea?”

Let me tell you.

A few days ago, I was at a Christmas party, just chatting with my nieces like usual.

Then one of them got very serious.

You know that look. Like… I-have-a-problem-and-you’re-the-adult serious.

She said,

“Julie, I LOVE spooky bags. Like… obsessed.But none of them fit my laptop, my textbooks, and my lunch.All I have is a plain black backpack.Do you have any spooky bags that are actually big enough for school?”

And for a second, my brain went blank.

My first thought was the boring adult one:

“Skulls probably aren’t for school, babe…”

Then I stopped myself.

Why not?

She still loves the gothic style. That’s her personality.

So I made the safe choice and got her the Lunacat backpack.

But after that… I couldn’t stop thinking.

Why don’t I take the possible designs from Skullette and turn them into a gothic-for-school collection?

So I started picking them one by one.

The “safe” designs.

The ones that still feel gothic—but work for everyday school life.

Bags that are big enough for laptops, books, lunch, chargers… all of it.

And here we are.

My top picks for gothic bags for school.

This little collection is for students who love gothic style.

For those who don’t want to look boring and want their bag to show who they are.

But if I’m being honest, I’m still stuck on one thing.

What about skulls?

Are skull bags too much for school…

or are they possible for students who aren’t afraid to stand out?

I really want to hear your thoughts.

Besides the “safe” designs I picked…

Which Skullette designs do YOU think could be possible to be in the gothic school bag collection?

Reply and tell me, please.

I really do read every reply.
