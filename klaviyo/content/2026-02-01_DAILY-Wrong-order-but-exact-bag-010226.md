This early morning.

I'm staring at my inbox with my third cup of coffee.

And there it is.

Subject: "I ordered wrong size"

My stomach drops.

Oh no. Here we go.

Look. I love my customers.

But when I see that subject line?

I know what's coming.

Someone's gonna need a return label. And I'll write seventeen different ways trying to make it sound sincere.

So I click open Monika's email. Deep breath.

And I start reading:

"After making an impulse purchase because I was tortured with the thought that it would be sold out by morning, I ordered the wrong size!!!"

Yep. Called it. I'm already mentally writing the return email in my head:

"Hi Monika, so sorry to hear that, here's your label, let me know if there's anything else—"

But then I keep reading:

"To my surprise, the smaller tote arrived and she's stunningly perfect. Fits perfectly in my locker at work."

I… Wait. What? I read it again.

She ordered the WRONG size. And it's... PERFECT?

I immediately screenshot this and send it to Alice.

Me:"ALICE. LOOK AT THIS."

Alice:"Is this a joke?"

Me:"NO IT'S REAL"

Alice:"That literally never happens."

Me:"I KNOW"

And when someone emails me"I ordered the wrong size"? That email never ends with good news.

But Monika is an exception?

She quickly bought her favorite bag at midnight because she was afraid it'd sell out.

Clicked the wrong size.

And somehow the universe just... handled it.

Like fate said,"Sweetie, I got you."

Here's the thing:Monika didn't overthink her wrong-size bag.

She just trusted her luck.And it worked out.

But now listen.

I'm NOT saying you should close your eyes, spin around, and randomly click a size.

(Please don't do that. We'll both end up crying into our coffee.)

Do your homework.

Check the measurements. Look at the photos. Read the product descriptions carefully.

But also sometimes? Don't overthink yourself into paralysis.

If you've been staring at a bag for three weeks trying to"decide"?

Maybe it's time to take a page from Monika's playbook.
