## Body
Hey witch,

Honestly, I should be sad right now.

But instead?I’m stupidly happy.Like grinning-at-my-screen happy.

Let me explain.

This morning, I opened my inbox and saw an email from Michelle Germano.

“I love the new collection! If I didn't get 4 bags and 4 wallets in the last 2 months (you killed me with the Black Friday deals!), I'd probably already bought Gravebloom and Blacklitany. I love the idea of an all over pattern (I already own Mournaire and it's so great). I'm obsessed with every bag I have from you — they’re amazing! Good quality and so roomy without looking bulky! I am a skull and rose girlie but every pattern you have is beautiful!”

Now… This is the kind of email that should make a founder sit down and cry into her keyboard.

Because the sad part is obvious:

If I launch another collection right now…a LOT of you will probably skip it.

Because many of you already went full feral during Black Friday and Christmas.

(And honestly? Respect.)

But the happy part?

You love and trust my designs.

You support them even when your wallets are still sore.

And that means more to me than I can even put into words.

But here’s the sentence in Michelle’s email that made my brain light up like a neon skull:

She mentioned the all-over pattern designs.

The repeating skulls. The roses.

The tiny icons layered everywhere like a secret language.

And that made me pause.

Because when I first created all-over pattern bags…

I honestly just thought they were pretty.

Because these patterns carry a very specific energy:

Classic. Luxurious. Quietly expensive.

(Yes, I’m basically saying “Skullette meets Louis Vuitton” 😂)

But truthfully?

They’re also the HARDEST designs to get right.

Every skull… every rose… every icon has to click together perfectly.

Not too busy — not too empty.

And choosing which icons even DESERVE to be repeated?

That’s the hardest part of all.

So after reading Michelle’s email, I had a thought:

Why am I guessing? Why not just ask you?

If you could design a Skullette all-over pattern,

what motif would you want to repeat?

Skulls? Roses? Cats? Bats?

Something unhinged I haven’t even thought of yet?

Reply and send me an image

of your favorite artwork or icon.

Seriously. I’ll read every message.

I’ll collect them one by one into my idea vault.

Imagine one day…you see your favorite symbol on a Skullette bag.

Wonderful, right?

And if you can’t quite imagine what I mean by“Skullette all-over pattern”…

Here’s one of my signatures —Mournaire:

Yeah. That energy.

Go haunt the shop while it’s still open.
