# 🧠 SKULLETTE — BRAND CONTEXT & WORKING MEMORY

---

## PURPOSE & CONTEXT

Duc works in email and SMS marketing for Skullette, a gothic leather handbag brand that targets women who embrace dark aesthetics year-round. The brand positions itself as creating **"armor for women who are told they're too much,"** selling skull-themed bags, wallets, and accessories with gothic hardware and wine-dark linings. Skullette operates on principles of **quality over trends** and **polarization over mass appeal**, actively repelling mainstream "Basic Brenda" customers while attracting "witches" who wear black in summer and read dark romance.

**Key team members:**
- **Julie** — Founder and brand voice
- **Alice** — Operations manager (known for her energetic sales reactions)
- **Jade** — Gothic designer

The brand universe centers on celebrating being "too much" rather than conforming, with handbags positioned as *warnings* rather than mere accessories. Success is measured through sales targets tied to team incentives, community building among gothic customers, and maintaining authentic brand voice that polarizes audiences effectively.

---

## CURRENT STATE

Duc is actively developing sophisticated promotional campaigns featuring:
- **Gamified pricing mechanics** (prices shuffling every 3 hours across multiple tiers)
- **Treasure hunt sales structures**
- **Tiered discount systems**
- **Challenge-based events** like "The Final 300" with narrative elements involving fictional supplier bets

Recent campaigns include **Friday the 13th** campaigns, **BOGO winter sales**, and narrative-driven events. Current campaigns incorporate real-time storytelling, segmented SMS marketing across different audience tiers, and email sequences that build FOMO while maintaining community celebration over high-pressure tactics.

The marketing approach emphasizes:
- Authentic brand voice over generic sales copy
- Realistic scenarios over fabricated drama
- Relationship-focused messaging (especially for high-value customers)
- Leading with urgent offers and immediate links rather than building up to them

---

## KEY LEARNINGS & PRINCIPLES

### What Works ✅
- Bold messaging that avoids alienating mockery of the female target audience
- Julie's authentic voice: dark, sassy, unapologetic with (parenthetical asides), CAPSLOCK emphasis, gothic terminology
- Actual scenes with dialogue
- Strong villain presence (Basic Brendas, mainstream beige culture)
- Confident rather than desperate positioning
- Clear offer communication in the **first third** of emails

### What Fails ❌
- Corporate language
- Missing brand personality
- Unrealistic timing scenarios
- Overly apologetic or explanatory content
- Validation-seeking "Please-Like-Me-itis" language
- The brand thrives on **polarization and indifference** toward whether customers buy

---

## APPROACH & PATTERNS

### Email Creation Workflow
1. Content development
2. Expert critique (often roleplaying as **Ben Settle** and **Daniel Throssell**)
3. Revision based on feedback
4. HTML formatting with specific styling
5. Subject line optimization

### Copywriting Methodology
- **Ben Settle** — Infotainment methodology
- **Daniel Throssell** — Scene stacking techniques
- Personality-driven copy that challenges readers rather than seeking consensus

### Campaign Development
- Start with the **most exciting elements** rather than building up to them
- Incorporate psychological goals for each message
- Create the impression that Julie is building and improving sale programs in real-time

### SMS Marketing
- Sophisticated audience segmentation:
  - Registered participants
  - High-engaged customers
  - Mass subscribers
- Distinct messaging approaches for each tier
- Consistent brand voice across all communications
- Text-to-join systems (e.g., texting "F13" to access private lists)
