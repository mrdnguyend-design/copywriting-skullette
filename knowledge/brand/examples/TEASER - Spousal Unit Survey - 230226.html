A few weeks ago, right after the survey went out, I got an email that made me set my coffee down.

Not because it was mean.

Not because it was weird.

(Okay — it was a LITTLE weird.)

Three words I have never seen in a customer email:

"My spousal unit."

"My spousal unit took this photo as an example."

I opened the attachment.

It was a spider.

Not a cartoon. Not a Pinterest save. Not a "hey wouldn't this be cool" screenshot.

A real, actual orb weaver on its web. Photographed by her "spousal unit." As a reference. Because she had design notes for us.

The email was from Helga.

Dr. Helga George, PhD.

English Editor at Southwestern Entomologist.

As in — this woman studies BUGS for a LIVING.

And she just peer-reviewed our spider bag.

I forwarded the email to Jade. Three dots. Then:

Jade: "Did a customer just send us entomological research?"

Me: "She has a PhD."

Jade: "...I think she knows more about our bag creatures than I do."

Me: "She definitely does."

Jade: "I'm going to go stare at our spider design for a while and feel inadequate."

(She did. For about 47 seconds. Then she opened Photoshop.)

Other brands pay agencies six figures for market research. We got ours free. From a bug scientist. With a spider photo. And a partner she refers to as her "spousal unit" without a hint of irony.

(I want to be Helga when I grow up.)

But here's the part that actually hit me.

Helga wrote: "I love my bag, although no one ever comments on it. I suspect they are being polite. This is a fairly conservative city."

Nobody comments.

She knows they see it. She knows they don't know what to say.

And she carries it anyway. Every day. In a city that would rather she didn't.

That's not fashion. That's a DECLARATION.

Because Helga doesn't need a stranger at the grocery store to tell her the bag is cool. She has a doctorate. She knows what she likes. And what she likes is a leather bag with a skull on it, in a town that thinks skulls are "a little morbid."

For the past few weeks, Jade and I have been going through every single reply from that survey. Every vote. Every design note. Every spousal-unit-photographed arachnid.

And we've been BUILDING.

7 new artworks. Designs we've never tried before. Things with wings. Things that crawl. Things that would make your Concerned Aunt clutch her pearls and whisper to your mother at Thanksgiving.

But we're not done deciding which ones survive.

The survey is still open.
