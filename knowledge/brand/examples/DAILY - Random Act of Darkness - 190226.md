**Subject Line:** "Everyone's giving something up. I'm giving in."
**Preview Text:** My Lent list looks a little different than yours.

---

6 AM. Phone glow, dog snoring, no lights because fluorescent before coffee should be a federal crime.

I'm scrolling. Third Lent post in a row.

"Giving up sugar for Lent! 🙏"

"No social media for 40 days! Pray for me!"

"Giving up online shopping until Easter!"

(That last one made me snort so hard my dog woke up. She's going to crack by Thursday. We both know it.)

And I'm lying there thinking — you know what I gave up a long time ago?

Apologizing for wearing all black to someone else's event. Stopped doing that in 2019 after I told my cousin's wedding photographer to work around me or use me as contrast.

Pretending beige is a color. It's not. It's a surrender. I don't negotiate with surrenders.

Saying "oh this old thing?" when someone compliments my bag. It is NOT old. I spent four days choosing it. I would die for it. And I will NOT pretend it was an accident.

And I was feeling pretty good about this list when I remembered the Karen incident.

Last Tuesday. Break room.

I'm minding my own business, pouring coffee into my skull mug — yes, I bring my own mug to work, yes, it has a skull on it, no, I will not be taking questions — when Karen-from-accounting walks in.

She does the thing.

You know the thing. The head tilt. Like a golden retriever who heard a weird noise.

"Don't you think skulls are a little... *morbid*?"

She said it like she was delivering bad news. Like she genuinely believed she was helping me.

I looked at her. I looked at her oatmeal-colored cardigan. I looked at the beige desk organizer visible through the break room door.

"Karen, that desk organizer is a cry for help."

Her mouth opened. Closed. Opened again. Nothing came out.

She hasn't tilted her head at me since.

(My mug and I left the break room unbothered. The mug winked at her on the way out. It didn't. But it felt like it did.)

And maybe you've been doing that too. A little. Here and there. Buying the safe bag instead of the one that actually looked like you. Throwing on a cardigan over the outfit you ACTUALLY wanted to wear.

But it's Lent now. Everybody's giving something up.

So here's what I'm giving up: pretending I should be less.

And here's what I'm giving IN to: the Bloodraven that's been sitting in my Today's Deal all morning, looking at me like it already knows it's coming home with somebody today.

Today's Deal is live. The Bloodraven is in there. A few others too — the ones with teeth.
