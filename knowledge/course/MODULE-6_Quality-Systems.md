## 6.1 — EMAIL QUALITY CHECKLIST (23 Items)

> Chạy checklist này TRƯỚC KHI publish bất kỳ email nào.

### Section 1: Voice & Persona Check (7 items)

- [ ] **Sounds like the Founder** — witchy best friend, NOT corporate marketer
- [ ] **5th-grade reading level** — punchy, short sentences, lots of spacing
- [ ] **CAPSLOCK used for drama** — at least 2-3 instances
- [ ] **(Parenthetical asides)** — inner thoughts in parentheses, at least 2-3
- [ ] **Dark humor present** — gothic metaphors, darkness references
- [ ] **No "Please-Like-Me-itis"** — no validation-seeking, no apologizing for selling
- [ ] **No corporate language** — no "we're excited to announce," no "valued customer"

### Section 2: Story & Craft Check (6 items)

- [ ] **Opens mid-action** — NOT "Hey witch!" or "Happy [holiday]!" or "I hope you're well"
- [ ] **Sensory details** — can you smell/hear/see the moment?
- [ ] **Specific details** — "third coffee at 9 AM" > "having coffee", "6 minutes" > "quickly"
- [ ] **Dialogue present** — actual conversations, not summaries (where applicable)
- [ ] **Show don't tell** — "her face went through three emotions" > "she was surprised"
- [ ] **Emotional arc** — vulnerability → revelation → pride → action

### Section 3: Structure Check (6 items)

- [ ] **Hook in first 3 lines** — reader decides to keep reading or delete
- [ ] **Bridge feels natural** — story → product transition earned, not forced
- [ ] **Broccoli is hidden** — product specs wrapped in story/personality, not naked
- [ ] **CTA is direct and unapologetic** — "Go scare the neighbors" > "Click here to shop"
- [ ] **P.S. adds value** — witty comment, scarcity, or tease (not just "P.S. Buy now")
- [ ] **Appropriate length** — long enough to tell the story, short enough to maintain tension

### Section 4: Brand Check (5 items)

- [ ] **Villain present** — Basic Brenda, Concerned Aunt, beige culture, or conformity referenced
- [ ] **Core values embodied** — "too much" celebration, dark aesthetics, quality over trends
- [ ] **Polarizing** — would repel Basic Brendas and attract Witches
- [ ] **Product = weapon/armor** — described as powerful, not just functional
- [ ] **Sign-off on brand** — "Stay spooky/wicked/weird," not "Best regards,"

### Section 5: Logic Check (5 items)

- [ ] **Character consistency** — Julie, Alice, Jamie, Jade all in-character
- [ ] **Timeline makes sense** — "this morning" matches actual send time
- [ ] **Scene believability** — could this actually happen in real life?
- [ ] **No accidental mockery** — bold without alienating the TARGET audience (women 30+)
- [ ] **Cultural sensitivity** — confident, not offensive toward own audience

### Section 6: Sale Email Specific (6 items — chỉ cho sale emails)

- [ ] **Offer in first third** — don't bury the deal behind a long story
- [ ] **Scarcity is specific** — "50 bags" not "limited stock"
- [ ] **Multiple CTAs** — at least 2-3 throughout, not just at the end
- [ ] **FOMO future-pacing** — paint the regret picture
- [ ] **No apologizing for selling** — SHAMELESS, confident, indifferent
- [ ] **Tiered offer clear** — if tiered pricing, each tier explained simply

### Scoring System

| Score | Action |
|-------|--------|
| All checks passed ✅ | → Ready to publish |
| 1-2 misses | → Minor revision, fix and re-check |
| 3+ misses | → Major revision needed, may need to re-brainstorm approach |

## 6.2 — THE CRITIQUE PROCESS (Expert Review Framework)

### 3-Pass Review

```
DRAFT V1
    ↓
PASS 1: Ben Settle Lens 🎰
    → Infotainment quality
    → Personality present?
    → Broccoli hidden?
    → CTA shameless?
    → Polarizing enough?
    ↓
PASS 2: Daniel Throssell Lens 🎭
    → Story quality
    → Scene stacking correct?
    → Hook strong enough?
    → Bridge earned?
    → Emotional arc complete?
    ↓
PASS 3: Logic & Brand Check ✅
    → Character consistency
    → Timeline accuracy
    → No accidental mockery
    → Brand standards met
    ↓
FINAL VERSION
```

### How to Run a Ben Settle Critique

**Ask these questions:**

1. "If I received this email from a stranger, would I keep reading past line 3?"
2. "Could I rip out the product mention and the email still entertains?"
3. "Is the CTA asking or commanding?"
4. "Would this email make Brenda uncomfortable?" (If no → not polarizing enough)
5. "Am I teaching or performing?"

### How to Run a Daniel Throssell Critique

**Ask these questions:**

1. "Can I see/hear/smell this scene?" (If not → add sensory details)
2. "Are there actual spoken words in quotes?" (If not → add dialogue)
3. "Does each scene connect to the next?" (If not → fix transitions)
4. "Is there a moment where I as the reader feel something?" (If not → add vulnerability)
5. "Would a cold reader — who has NEVER read a previous email — understand and enjoy this?" (Critical!)

## 6.3 — AI-ISM AUDIT (Proofread Check)

### Banned Patterns

Sau khi viết xong (especially nếu dùng AI assist), audit cho các patterns sau:

| Pattern | Ví dụ | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| **Em dash overuse** | "She walked — no, ran — to the door" | Max 2-3 per email |
| **"Delve into"** | "Let's delve into..." | Delete or replace |
| **"It's worth noting"** | "It's worth noting that..." | Cut entirely |
| **"In terms of"** | "In terms of quality..." | Rephrase |
| **"Navigating"** | "Navigating the..." | Use simpler verb |
| **"Tapestry/landscape/ecosystem"** | "The landscape of..." | Be specific |
| **"Leverage"** | "We leverage..." | "We use" |
| **"I'd be happy to"** | "I'd be happy to help..." | Too corporate |
| **Lists of 3 with escalation** | "bigger, bolder, more beautiful" | Break the pattern |
| **Overly smooth transitions** | Every paragraph flows "perfectly" | Add some jarring |

### The "Read It Out Loud" Test

> Đọc to email. Nếu bạn nghe giống ROBOT hoặc giống ESSAY → rewrite phần đó.
> Nếu bạn nghe giống đang KỂ CHUYỆN cho bạn bè → pass.

## 6.4 — KAIZEN LOG: CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT SYSTEM

### Cách hoạt động

Sau MỖI email published → ghi nhận 1 entry:

```
### Entry #[number] — [date]
**Email:** [name]
**Type:** [Daily/Sale/Teaser/etc]
**Product:** [what was promoted]
**Rating:** ⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**What worked (KEEP DOING):**
1. [Technique + why it worked]
2. [Element + evidence]

**What needed revision (AVOID NEXT TIME):**
1. [Issue + how it was fixed]

**Key learnings → UPDATE WORKFLOW:**
1. [New pattern/rule to add to system]
```

### Pattern Tracker

Theo dõi THEO THỜI GIAN để thấy patterns:

| Category | Tracker |
|----------|---------|
| **Laws hiệu quả nhất** | Law # + frequency used + effectiveness rating |
| **Mechanisms hiệu quả nhất** | Seinfeld/Scene Stack/Simplicio + notes |
| **Hooks hiệu quả nhất** | Hook type + example + open rate |
| **Subject line patterns** | Formula + performance data |

### Review Schedule

| Milestone | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Every 5 entries | Update Pattern Tracker |
| Every 10 entries | Review toàn bộ → update workflows & instructions |
| Every 20 entries | Deep review → add/remove Laws, mechanisms, examples |

## 6.6 — COMPLETE CONTENT PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### End-to-End Process

```
PHASE 1: RESEARCH (15 min)
├── Check customer emails/reviews for inspiration
├── Check cultural calendar / trending topics
├── Brainstorm 3 angles
└── Pick winning angle

PHASE 2: DRAFT (30-45 min)
├── Write hook first (3 lines)
├── Build story/scenes
├── Write bridge
├── Add product/offer
├── Write CTA + P.S.
└── Write 5 subject line options

PHASE 3: CRITIQUE (10 min)
├── Ben Settle lens pass
├── Daniel Throssell lens pass
└── Note revisions needed

PHASE 4: REVISION (15-20 min)
├── Fix identified issues
├── Run Quality Checklist (23 items)
└── Re-read once more

PHASE 5: AI-ISM AUDIT (5 min)
├── Check banned patterns
├── Read out loud test
└── Fix any robot-sounding parts

PHASE 6: FORMAT (10 min)
├── HTML template conversion
├── Image placement
├── CTA button styling
└── Preview text finalization

PHASE 7: PUBLISH
├── Schedule in Klaviyo
├── Double-check audience segment
└── Verify links

PHASE 8: KAIZEN (5 min post-send)
├── Log entry in Kaizen Log
├── Note what worked / what didn't
└── Update Pattern Tracker (every 5 entries)
```

**Total time per daily email: ~90-120 minutes**
**Total time per sale email: ~120-180 minutes** (higher stakes = more revision)
