# Skullette Writing Patterns — Extracted from 225 Klaviyo Campaigns
> Actual patterns from real sent emails. Evidence-based, not theoretical.
> Read before writing. Cross-ref with `content-standards.md` for quality criteria.

## 1. Julie's Voice Patterns

### Core Persona
- **Warm but unapologetic** — never asks permission to be dark
- **Conversational** — friend texting at 2 AM, not a newsletter
- **Parenthetical asides** — "(I don't actually reply this. Alice won't let me.)"
- **CAPSLOCK for emphasis** — sparingly, at emotional peaks: "THIS. IS. MY. DAUGHTER."
- **Self-deprecating humor** — "I stare at the email. Then I open the weather map."
- **Indifferent to approval** — "Brenda would never." (said with zero concern)

### Voice Markers (from top 15 emails)
- **Sign-off:** "Talk soon, Julie" or "Talk soon, / Julie / Founder, Skullette"
- **Greeting:** "Hey {{ first_name }}," — never "Dear" or "Hi there!"
- **Asides:** Parentheses for inner voice, not italics
- **Transitions:** "Here's the thing:" — Julie's signature pivot phrase
- **Emphasis:** Bold for key emotional beats, CAPSLOCK for peaks

## 3. Structural Patterns

### The "Julie Story Arc" (Daily Emails)
*Used by 90 daily emails. Best at: Snow Storm 39.8%, Sonja Spooky Daughter 25.8%*

```
1. SCENE SETTER — Specific moment: time, place, sensory detail
   "Got this gem in my inbox this morning..."
2. THE HOOK — Something unexpected disrupts the moment
   "...from a customer I'll call 'Karen'"
3. THE STORY — Julie reacts, investigates, reflects
   (Multiple scenes OK: email → weather map → USPS dashboard)
4. THE TURN — Gap between expectation and reality
   "Half the country is buried under snow."
5. THE PIVOT — Natural bridge to product/brand meaning
   "If you have a Skullette order in transit..."
6. THE CTA — Matches emotional altitude
7. SIGN-OFF + P.S. — Thematically connected
```

### The "Urgency Machine" (Sale Emails)
*Used by 93 sale emails. Best at: Weekend Specials 32.7%, Friday 13th 27.4%*

```
1. CONTEXT BLOCK — Offer + scarcity upfront (first 3-5 lines)
2. STATUS UPDATE — What's selling, what's left
3. SOCIAL PROOF — Customer reactions (raw quotes)
4. DEAL RECAP — Specific numbers, specific products
5. DIRECT CTA — Command voice, multiple CTAs OK
6. DEADLINE — Specific time, not vague
```

### The "Anticipation Engine" (Teaser Emails)
*Used by 35 teasers. Best at: Bag Charm Survey 31.9%*

```
1. MYSTERY — Hint at what's coming without full reveal
2. INSIDER ENERGY — "You're seeing this before anyone else"
3. INTERACTIVE — Surveys/polls dramatically boost engagement
4. CURIOSITY GAP — Each teaser reveals one new layer
5. CTA — Register / Save date / Join list
```

## 4. Subject Line Patterns

### Top 10 Subject Lines by Open Rate (min 20K recipients)

| # | Subject | Open Rate | Type | Pattern |
|---|---------|-----------|------|---------|
| 1 | "USPS notice: your order may arrive a late" | 39.8% | Daily | Fake-official + typo |
| 2 | "The Weekend Specials Day 1" | 32.7% | Sale | Direct series title |
| 3 | "Bag Charm Survey" (+ interactive content) | 31.9% | Teaser | Interactive promise |
| 4 | "Fast Fashion" | 30.5% | Daily | Industry hot take |
| 5 | "Noxaria" | 27.7% | Daily | Product name only (mystery) |

### Formulas That Work
- **Fake-official:** "USPS notice:", "[Announcement]" — borrows authority
- **Product name only:** "Noxaria", "BloodRose" — pure curiosity
- **Customer quote:** "Perfect Gift for my Spooky Daughter" — social proof
- **Question:** "Wait... Bloodrose is GONE?" — engagement trigger
- **Industry take:** "Fast Fashion" — hot topic relevance

### What to Avoid
- **Pure urgency:** "LAST CHANCE" subjects = 22.6% avg (worst hook type)
- **Long subjects:** >50 chars truncate on mobile
- **Generic:** "New collection" or "Sale update" = boring

## 5. P.S. Patterns

### 5 Jobs for P.S.
1. **Scarcity reminder** — "P.S. Only 12 left at this price."
2. **Secondary CTA** — "P.S. Forward this to your Sonja-mom."
3. **Indifference flex** — "P.S. If this isn't for you, no worries. Brenda has options."
4. **Next tease** — "P.S. Wait until you see what Jade's working on..."
5. **Community moment** — "P.S. [Customer name], if you're reading this — thank you."

### Rule: P.S. must be thematically connected to the email's story.

## 6. Anti-Patterns (from Bottom 10 Performers)

| Pattern | Evidence | Avg Open Rate |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Long teaser sequences (5+) | BFBF teasers 2-6 | ~19.7% |
| SMS recruitment in email | "Why SMS" | 19.6% |
| Self-referential post-mortems | "Failed Salesperson" | 19.9% |
| Generic sale updates | Multiple Day 2/3 emails | 20.0% |

**Lesson:** Every email must earn its open independently. Series fatigue is real.
