# SKULLETTE CONTENT TAXONOMY
## The Classification System for All Broadcasts (Email + SMS)

> **Mục đích:** Định nghĩa TẠI SAO viết, KHÔNG PHẢI viết cái gì.
> Đọc file này trước khi plan, trước khi viết, trước khi review bất kỳ content nào.

---

## PART 1: CONTENT PURPOSES

### 1A — Campaign Sequence Emails

Emails thuộc một series có mục đích rõ ràng. Mỗi email có **Campaign Job** riêng:

| Job | Mục đích | Hook | Pivot | CTA |
|-----|---------|------|-------|-----|
| **ANNOUNCE** | Tạo awareness về event/sale sắp tới | Mystery/tease | "Something's coming..." | Đăng ký / Stay tuned |
| **JUSTIFY** | Giải thích tại sao deal này xứng đáng | Story chứng minh quality | "That's why this bag is different" | Shop link |
| **PROVE** | Social proof + testimonials | Customer witch story | "She's not the only one" | Browse testimonials |
| **TEACH** | Educate về product/brand story | Origin story, design process | "Jade spent 6 months on this" | Product page |
| **PREPARE** | Build anticipation trước launch | Countdown, insider info | "Set your witch alarms" | Register/join list |
| **CLOSE** | Last chance urgency | Scarcity reality | "After tonight, that's it" | Final CTA |
| **LIVE** | Real-time updates trong sale | Live chaos energy | Current status | Hunt link |
| **THANK** | Post-campaign community celebration | Witch wins celebration | "We did it together" | Community/next event |

**Campaign Types:**
- **Gamified** — Prices shuffle every X hours, tiers, treasure hunt mechanics
- **Flash/Themed** — Friday 13th, Halloween, seasonal events (short windows)
- **Mission** — Team challenges, sales goals with narrative stakes
- **Clearance** — End-of-season, moving inventory, honest framing

---

### 1B — Daily Broadcast Emails

Non-campaign emails sent regularly. Driven by **Story Sources:**

| Story Source | Định nghĩa | Ví dụ cho Skullette |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Gothic Mundane** | Mundane daily moment made darkly funny | Coffee mug has a skull on it, coworker asks about "the phase", buying candles again |
| **Witch Victory** | Customer/community member wins | Witch wears bag to corporate job, gets compliments from unexpected people |
| **Basic Brenda Encounter** | Real or fictional encounter với mainstream conformity | Brenda at the office asking about the "funeral bag", concerned aunt at Thanksgiving |
| **Product Origin** | Behind-the-scenes: design, materials, creation story | Jade's obsession with a specific hardware finish, wine-dark lining backstory |
| **Cultural Darkness** | Gothic/dark culture moment — book, show, music, season | New dark romance novel, October energy, full moon energy |
| **Julie Personal** | Founder's own life, embarrassing moments, observations | Bought 7 candles again, neighborhood reaction to Halloween decorations in March |
| **Industry Commentary** | Fashion industry, luxury market, "mainstream" bags — mockery | Beige "it bag" season, fast fashion skull knock-offs |

**Rule:** Rotate Story Sources across the week. No same source two days in a row.

## PART 2: CONTENT PILLARS

### 2A — Email Pillars (4)

Every email falls primarily into one pillar. Combinations allowed (max 2).

#### PILLAR 1: Gothic Infotainment
**Definition:** Story-first email where entertainment and brand voice DO the selling.
**When to use:** Daily sends, non-urgent, building relationship
**Structure:** Scene → Turn → Emotional moment → Pivot → Product
**Tone:** 100% Julie, playful chaos, sensory richness
**Example hook:** "I'm at the farmer's market. In my Moonreaver. And this woman just stopped mid-stride and stared at me like I was carrying a weapon. (I was.)"

#### PILLAR 2: The Witch's Arsenal
**Definition:** Product-focused with gothic framing. Shows HOW the product fits witch life.
**When to use:** New launches, product spotlights, after PROVE/TEACH campaign emails
**Structure:** Context → Product as solution → Features through gothic lens → CTA
**Tone:** Confident, sensory, "armor" language
**Example hook:** "The Bloodraven isn't a bag. It's the physical manifestation of every time you refused to apologize for existing."

#### PILLAR 3: Coven Chronicles
**Definition:** Community-driven. Customer wins, testimonials, "you're not alone" energy.
**When to use:** After campaigns, trust-building, low-pressure sends
**Structure:** Witch's story (raw words) → Julie's reaction → Community invitation → Product
**Tone:** Warm but not soft, celebratory, inclusive
**Example hook:** "I got an email from a witch in Ohio last Tuesday at 11:47 PM. She started it with: 'Julie, you're going to lose your mind.'"

#### PILLAR 4: Darkness vs. Beige
**Definition:** Polarization email. Julie vs. mainstream culture/expectations.
**When to use:** When engagement is low, new subscriber welcome, seasonal contrast moments
**Structure:** Beige world moment → Julie's reaction → Why we're different → Product as symbol
**Tone:** Sassy, indifferent, mockery of archetype (never of real people)
**Example hook:** "My neighbor got a new bag last week. Taupe. With a gold chain. I have nothing to say. (I have several things to say.)"

### 2B — SMS Pillars (4)

#### SMS PILLAR 1: Live Chaos
**Definition:** Real-time energy, timestamps, team reactions, specific numbers
**When to use:** Active sale events, price shuffle windows, live campaigns
**Tone:** Chaos mode, CAPSLOCK, parenthetical asides, Julie "losing control"
**Example:** "2:13 PM UPDATE — TWENTY THREE bags just vanished in 8 minutes. Alice is making a noise I've never heard before. Hunt here → [LINK]"

#### SMS PILLAR 2: Insider Access
**Definition:** "You're in the coven" energy — special info, early access, exclusive details
**When to use:** REG tier, pre-launch teasers, VIP moments
**Tone:** Conspiratorial, intimate, "just between us"
**Example:** "We're not supposed to announce this until tomorrow. But you're already in. New drop preview → [LINK]"

#### SMS PILLAR 3: Witch Check-in
**Definition:** Short, personality-first, relationship touchpoint
**When to use:** HIGH tier engagement, non-sale periods, re-engagement
**Tone:** Casual, funny, personal — like a text from a friend
**Example:** "Genuinely asking: how many black bags is too many? (Asking for myself. The answer is probably never.) Current obsessions → [LINK]"

#### SMS PILLAR 4: The Dark Offer
**Definition:** Direct deal SMS — offer first, minimal context, clear CTA
**When to use:** MASS tier, flash deals, simple promotions
**Tone:** Direct, benefit-first, just enough personality to not sound corporate
**Example:** "Friday the 13th: 13% off everything. Today only. No code needed. Go → [LINK] — Julie"

---

**SMS Pillar Combinations:**
- Live Chaos + Insider Access = REG tier during active sale
- Witch Check-in + Dark Offer = HIGH tier flash promotion
- Dark Offer alone = MASS tier simple sends
- Insider Access alone = Teasers for REG before launch

## PART 3: TECHNIQUES

### 3A — Hook Patterns (8 types)

| Pattern | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Gothic Mundane** | Ordinary moment + dark twist | "I'm at the grocery store. In November. Full goth. A child pointed at me." |
| **Witchy Confession** | Julie admits something embarrassing or obsessive | "I bought another skull. The fifth this month. I regret nothing." |
| **Brenda Encounter** | Real or fictional clash with beige culture | "My coworker just asked if my bag was 'from a Halloween store.' I need a moment." |
| **Customer Drop** | Open with raw customer words | "Got this at 11:48 PM on a Tuesday: 'Julie, I wore it to my corporate job. Six compliments. Zero apologies.'" |
| **Mystery Statement** | Bold or strange claim that demands explanation | "I almost didn't make the Raven Tote. Almost." |
| **Live Scene** | Jump into action mid-moment | "Three minutes into the sale and Alice is already pacing. I'm eating popcorn." |
| **Dark Question** | Question that creates immediate curiosity | "Have you ever walked into a room and wanted everyone to slightly fear your bag?" |
| **The Contrast** | Juxtapose two worlds (dark vs beige) | "Somewhere right now, Basic Brenda is at Nordstrom buying something champagne-colored. We are not her." |

---

### 3B — Pivot Methods

The bridge from story → product. Must feel earned, not forced.

| Pivot | When to use | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **The Realization** | After emotional moment | "That's when I realized — the bag isn't decoration. It's communication." |
| **The Direct Turn** | Mid-pace, Ben Settle style | "Here's the thing." (hard cut to product) |
| **The Quote** | Customer words make the pivot | "'This bag made me feel like I could take over the world.' That's Jade's whole brief." |
| **The Parallel** | Story mirrors product purpose | "She refused to blend in. The Moonreaver was designed for exactly that." |

---

### 3C — CTA Styles

| Style | Tone | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Command** | Direct, confident | "Go wield this." / "Claim your armor." |
| **Challenge** | Polarizing, bet they won't | "If you're brave enough." |
| **Indifference** | Classic Julie | "Or don't. I won't beg." |
| **Urgency** | Specific, not vague | "47 left. After that, it's gone for good." |
| **Community** | Invitation to the coven | "Join the rest of your coven." |

## PART 4: DECISION TREES

### Daily Email Decision Tree

```
Start: What is today's goal?

→ NO active campaign
   └─ Which Story Source hasn't been used in 2 days?
      → Gothic Mundane / Julie Personal → Pillar 1 (Gothic Infotainment)
      → Witch Victory / Customer → Pillar 3 (Coven Chronicles)
      → Basic Brenda Encounter → Pillar 4 (Darkness vs Beige)
      → Product Origin / Cultural → Pillar 2 (Witch's Arsenal)

→ ACTIVE campaign
   └─ What's the campaign job today?
      → ANNOUNCE / PREPARE / TEACH → Pillars 1 or 2
      → PROVE / THANK → Pillar 3
      → CLOSE / LIVE → Pillar 4 or direct sale format
      → JUSTIFY → Pillar 2 (product-focused)
```

### SMS Decision Tree

```
Start: Which audience tier?

→ REG (campaign registered insiders)
   └─ Active sale → SMS Pillar 1 (Live Chaos)
   └─ Pre-launch → SMS Pillar 2 (Insider Access)

→ HIGH (active openers)
   └─ Sale event → Pillar 1 or 2
   └─ Non-sale → Pillar 3 (Witch Check-in) or 4 (Dark Offer)

→ MASS (full list)
   └─ Always → Pillar 4 (Dark Offer) — direct, minimal context
```

## PART 5: ANTI-PATTERNS (25)

Things that kill Skullette content. Never do these.

**Voice Anti-Patterns:**
1. Starting with "Hey witch!" — generic opener, not a scene
2. "I hope this finds you well" — corporate death sentence
3. "Our bags are high quality" — tell, don't show
4. Explaining/justifying gothic lifestyle — we don't explain ourselves
5. Seeking validation ("I hope you like this") — Please-Like-Me-itis
6. Over-apologizing for anything — darkness never apologizes
7. "As women, we..." — beige feminist corporate speak
8. Triple-And anaphora ("And she... And she... And she...")

**Story Anti-Patterns:**
9. Julie not IN the scene — narrating from outside, not inhabiting
10. Fabricated scenarios that couldn't happen in real life
11. Summarizing instead of showing: "She was very surprised" > show the face
12. Timeline impossibilities — "this morning" in evening sends
13. Over-explaining the joke/emotion — if you explain it, it's dead
14. Brenda used cruelly instead of with indifference — we mock the archetype, not people

**Sales Anti-Patterns:**
15. Burying the offer past halfway in sale emails
16. Vague scarcity ("limited stock", "selling fast") — always use specific numbers
17. Apologizing for promoting a sale — never say "I know you get a lot of emails"
18. Feature list after emotional story — product section stays in scene, not listicle
19. Single CTA only in sale emails — multiple CTAs needed

**SMS Anti-Patterns:**
20. Paragraphs in SMS — one thought per line
21. "VIP/EXCLUSIVE" language — too corporate, too salesy
22. No personality in MASS tier SMS — even 40 words needs Julie's voice
23. Vague timestamps ("later today") — always specific ("prices shuffle at 1:13 PM")

**AI-Ism Anti-Patterns:**
24. Em dash overuse (max 2-3 per email) — AI fingerprint
25. "Tapestry of darkness", "embrace your inner witch", "journey" — purple prose AI vocabulary

## PART 6: CHARACTER SYSTEM

### Primary Character Rules

**Julie (every email):**
- First-person always, in every email
- Must be physically IN the scene (not watching from outside)
- Voice markers: (parenthetical inner thoughts), CAPSLOCK for emphasis, short punchy sentences
- Never seeks approval. Never justifies the brand aesthetic. Never explains being "too much."
- Darkness is casual, not performed — like it's just Tuesday

**Alice (supporting role):**
- Use in: operations stories, "how we pulled this off" moments, chaos escalation
- Energetic reactions ("Alice just screamed across the building")
- Color-coded spreadsheets in black and deep purple — let this be visual detail when used
- Never more prominent than Julie in any email

**Jade (supporting role):**
- Use in: design stories, new product reveals, quality/craftsmanship moments
- Design philosophy language: "every buckle has meaning", "wine-dark linings are non-negotiable"
- The "gothic aesthetic architect" — never use generic design language

**Basic Brenda:**
- Foil character for polarization emails and Simplicio dialogues
- Indifferent dismissal, not cruelty — "she can stay in her beige world, we'll stay in ours"
- NEVER mock real specific people — Brenda is an archetype
- Appearance frequency: max 2x/week across email + SMS

**The Witch (customer):**
- Always use raw customer words when quoting
- Her victories belong to the whole coven, not just her
- Never sanitize or "professionalize" her language

### Character Rotation Rules
- Julie: every send (non-negotiable)
- Alice or Jade: max 3x/week combined
- Basic Brenda: max 2x/week
- Customer voice: at least 1x/week (minimum)

- Basic Brenda appears max 2x/week (Thu + 1 other)

During active campaigns, campaign job takes priority over daily Story Source distribution.
Daily Story Source fills gaps between campaign emails (non-campaign days).
