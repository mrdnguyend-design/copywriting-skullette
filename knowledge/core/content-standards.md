# SKULLETTE CONTENT STANDARDS
## Quality Criteria for Evaluating All Broadcasts

> **Mục đích:** Đánh giá chất lượng, KHÔNG PHẢI hướng dẫn viết.
> Dùng file này khi review, critique, hoặc self-check bất kỳ draft nào.

---

## PRIORITY ORDER

Khi đánh giá một email/SMS, check theo thứ tự này:

```
1. VOICE — Đây có nghe như Julie không?
2. SUBJECT LINE — Có mở email ra không?
3. STORY — Có scene thực sự không?
4. HOOK — Dòng đầu có stop the scroll không?
5. PIVOT — Bridge story→product có earned không?
6. CTA — Có unapologetic và specific không?
7. P.S. — Có làm việc gì không?
```

**Lý do:** Voice failure ở bước 1 → mọi thứ sau đều vô nghĩa.
Subject line failure → email không được mở → content không quan trọng.

---

## PART 1: VOICE STANDARDS

### The Julie Test (Pass/Fail)

Read the email aloud. Ask:

- [ ] Would a witchy best friend at 2 AM write this?
- [ ] Is there at least one (parenthetical aside)?
- [ ] Is there CAPSLOCK emphasis used sparingly (1-3 instances)?
- [ ] Does the opening drop us into a scene — not a greeting?
- [ ] Is Julie physically present in the story (not narrating from outside)?
- [ ] Zero apologies for darkness, gothic aesthetic, or having opinions?
- [ ] Zero corporate language ("our valued customers", "quality craftsmanship", "at this time")?
- [ ] Zero validation-seeking ("I hope you love this", "what do you think?")?

**Scoring:**
- 8/8 passes = Voice ✅ Authentic
- 6-7/8 = Voice ⚠️ Minor polish needed
- <6/8 = Voice ❌ Rewrite required — everything else is secondary

---

## PART 2: SUBJECT LINE STANDARDS

### What Makes a Skullette Subject Line

**Purpose:** Open rate. Not description. Not summary.

| Type | When to use | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Curiosity/Dark** | Daily emails | "I shouldn't have looked at her bag" |
| **Confession/Personal** | Julie-voice emails | "I bought another one. Obviously." |
| **Weird/Specific** | Engagement emails | "The woman at Kroger is still staring" |
| **Threat/Warning** | Sale emails | "This one's almost gone" |
| **Direct Benefit** | Flash sale, time-sensitive | "40% off ends at midnight" |

**Rules:**
- Under 50 characters preferred (mobile preview)
- No emojis unless the email itself uses them
- Never start with "Hey" or the product name
- Dark/weird > generic promotional
- Preview text = COMPLEMENTS subject (never repeats it)

**Red Flags:**
- "You won't want to miss this" — vague, corporate
- "[PRODUCT NAME] — now on sale" — description, not hook
- "Important update from Skullette" — corporate death
- All caps subject line — cheap urgency

## PART 3: STORY STANDARDS

### Scene Requirements (Email)

A story in a Skullette email must have:

1. **Location** — Where is Julie (or the customer)? Specific, not vague.
   - ✅ "At the farmer's market, November, freezing"
   - ❌ "The other day I was out"

2. **Sensory Detail** — At least 2 sensory anchors:
   - Smell: old books, rain, extinguished candles, leather, coffee, incense
   - Sound: combat boots on pavement, thunder, cat's chaos, Alice screaming
   - Visual: specific colors (wine-dark, skull hardware, matte black), texture

3. **A Turn** — The gap between expectation and reality. No turn = no scene.
   - Setup: normal situation
   - Turn: something unexpected happens
   - Reaction: Julie's response (internal or external)

4. **Julie's Inner Life** — (parenthetical reactions) make her human.
   - "I would die for it. (I actually might.)"

5. **Believability** — Could this actually happen? No fabricated drama.
   - ✅ Neighbor commented on the skeleton decoration
   - ❌ Jade flew from Paris specifically to show Julie this hardware

**Scoring:**
- 5/5 = Story ✅ Vivid
- 3-4/5 = Story ⚠️ Add missing elements
- <3/5 = Story ❌ No scene — summarizing only

## PART 4: HOOK STANDARDS

### The 3-Second Rule

Read the first 3 lines. Ask: Would a cold reader keep going?

**Hook must:**
- Drop into action or a specific moment (not a greeting)
- Create a question in the reader's mind ("what happens next?")
- Sound unmistakably like Julie

**Hook must NOT:**
- Start with "Hey witch!" / "Happy [holiday]!" / "I hope you..."
- Summarize what the email is about
- Be a generic observation

**Hook Quality Grades:**

| Grade | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| A | Specific, sensory, creates immediate curiosity or dark humor |
| B | Good voice but lacks sensory specificity or clear hook |
| C | Voice present but generic opener, no hook |
| F | Greeting, corporate opener, or summarizes the email |

---

## PART 5: PIVOT STANDARDS

### When the Bridge Works

The pivot (story → product) must feel EARNED, not bolted on.

**Test:** Remove the product mention. Does the story still feel complete?
- If yes: pivot is NOT earned (story and product are parallel universes)
- If no: pivot is earned (story creates the need for the product)

**Pivot Quality:**

| Grade | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| A | Story creates emotional need that product answers perfectly |
| B | Bridge is smooth but connection slightly logical (not emotional) |
| C | "Anyway, this week we have a sale" — story and product disconnected |
| F | No pivot — story ends, product section begins with no connection |

## PART 6: CTA STANDARDS

### Skullette CTA Rules

**For daily emails:**
- One primary CTA (not buried, not repeated 5 times)
- Command voice: "Go wield this." / "Claim yours." / "Hunt it here."
- Optional: indifference CTA in P.S. ("Or don't. I won't beg.")

**For sale emails:**
- First CTA in the first third of the email
- Multiple CTAs (every major section)
- Specific urgency: number of items left OR exact deadline
- No vague language: "limited quantities" → "47 left after this morning"

**CTA Red Flags:**
- "Click here to learn more" — zero personality
- "Shop now!" — corporate
- "Hurry, limited stock!" — vague urgency
- CTA only at the end of a long sale email

---

## PART 7: P.S. STANDARDS

### What P.S. Should Do

The P.S. is read by people who skim. It must work as a standalone unit.

**P.S. jobs (pick one):**
1. **Scarcity reminder** — Specific number + deadline
2. **Secondary CTA** — Different product or angle
3. **Indifference close** — Julie's wit, unapologetic ending
4. **Future tease** — "Something's coming Thursday"
5. **Community call** — Forward this to a witch who needs armor

**P.S. Red Flags:**
- Generic: "Thanks for reading!"
- Repeats what was already said
- Starts with "Also..."
- Missing entirely from sale emails (always include scarcity P.S.)

## PART 8: SMS STANDARDS

### SMS-Specific Checks

- [ ] One thought per line — no paragraphs
- [ ] Offer in first 2 lines for all tiers (no exceptions)
- [ ] Length appropriate for tier (REG: up to 300w, HIGH: 80-150w, MASS: 40-80w)
- [ ] CAPSLOCK for emphasis, not every line
- [ ] For live-event SMS: timestamp in the message
- [ ] Specific numbers: "16 bags" not "selling fast"
- [ ] No "VIP" / "EXCLUSIVE" language
- [ ] CTA link present
- [ ] Sounds like Julie, not a text alert

---

## PART 9: PRE-SEND CHECKLIST

### Email Pre-Send
- [ ] Voice test passed (8/8 Julie criteria)
- [ ] Subject line: under 50 chars, curiosity/dark style
- [ ] Preview text: complements, doesn't repeat subject
- [ ] Hook: drops into action, no greeting opener
- [ ] Scene: location + 2 sensory details + turn + Julie's inner life
- [ ] Pivot: story creates need for product
- [ ] CTA: command voice, specific urgency if sale
- [ ] P.S.: scarcity or wit (not generic)
- [ ] No anti-patterns triggered (check list of 25)
- [ ] Character rotation: who's appeared this week? Is Brenda overused?
- [ ] HTML formatted and tested

### SMS Pre-Send
- [ ] Offer/update in first 2 lines
- [ ] Length correct for tier
- [ ] Specific numbers (not vague)
- [ ] Link present
- [ ] Julie's voice present
- [ ] Timestamp if live-event

### Campaign Pre-Send
- [ ] Campaign Job correct for this email's position in sequence
- [ ] Sequence context: what did last email say? What does next email need?
- [ ] Scarcity: numbers are accurate to actual inventory
- [ ] Timeline: all timestamps/deadlines are accurate

## PART 10: WHEN TO BREAK RULES

Some rules exist for specific reasons. When context changes, the rule may shift.

| Rule | When to break it |
|------|-----------------|
| "Offer in first third" | Pure relationship email (no offer — intentionally) |
| "No greeting opener" | Very personal email (rare — Julie's real vulnerability moment) |
| "Indifference CTA" | When genuine urgency exists (Final 300, true last-chance) |
| "Max 2 Brenda mentions/week" | Themed campaigns (Halloween, anti-fast-fashion) |
| "One CTA daily email" | If email naturally has 2 products (bundle) |

**Decision rule:** Break the rule only when breaking it SERVES the witch — not when it makes the copy easier to write.
