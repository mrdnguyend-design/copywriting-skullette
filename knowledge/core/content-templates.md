# PART 1: EMAIL STRUCTURES

## 1A. Email Anatomy (shared across all types)

```
SUBJECT LINE          — Curiosity + specificity, <50 chars. Julie's voice.
PREVIEW TEXT          — Complements subject, adds context. NOT just first line of email.
─────────────────────
CONTEXT BLOCK         — (Sale/Campaign only) Offer summary in 3-5 lines. Above the story.
─────────────────────
HOOK (3-5 lines)      — Mid-action scene OR quote. Reader knows WHAT in 5 lines.
STORY (60-70%)        — Julie IN the scene. TURN point. Sensory details. Emotional arc.
PIVOT (2-4 lines)     — Invisible seam from story → product. Story creates NEED for product.
─────────────────────
CTA                   — Single action, matches emotional altitude. Command voice.
SIGN-OFF              — "Talk soon, / Julie" or variant
P.S.                  — Thematically connected. 1 clear purpose.
```

### Annotated Walkthrough: "Snow Storm" (39.8% open rate — all-time best)

```
SUBJECT: "USPS notice: your order may arrive a late"
↑ Curiosity (what happened?) + specificity (USPS) + Julie's casual typo style

PREVIEW: "Not lost. Not forgotten. Just because..."
↑ Complements subject, doesn't repeat it

HOOK: Got this gem in my inbox this morning from a customer I'll call "Karen":
"It's been FOUR DAYS and my order still hasn't arrived..."
↑ Opens with customer quote (quote_open hook). Immediate tension.

STORY: Julie stares at email → opens weather map → half country buried in snow →
Alice won't let her reply what she wants → USPS dashboard all "Delayed" →
Karen doesn't understand → HERE'S THE THING transition
↑ Julie IN scene. TURN = gap between Karen's rage and reality (snow). Show don't tell.
  Alice appears naturally. Parenthetical aside = fourth-wall break.

PIVOT: "If you have a Skullette order in transit... it's probably moving slower."
↑ Natural bridge from story to product/service. Not forced.

CTA: Check your tracking / Shop anyway
SIGN-OFF: Talk soon, Julie
P.S.: (weather-related humor — thematically connected)
```

## 1B. Structure by Campaign Job

### ANNOUNCE
```
Subject: Mystery/tease (don't reveal the deal)
Hook: Scene of something happening behind the scenes
Story: Julie discovers/plans something → hints → builds curiosity
Pivot: "I can't tell you everything yet, but..."
CTA: Register / Save the date / Join the list
```

### JUSTIFY
```
Subject: Value proposition hint
Hook: Customer scene OR product origin moment
Story: Why this product/deal is worth it → quality evidence → craftsmanship
Pivot: "That's why [product] isn't just another [category]"
CTA: Shop / Learn more
```

### PROVE
```
Subject: Customer quote or reaction
Hook: Customer's exact words (raw, with typos)
Story: Julie reads the review → her reaction → what it means
Pivot: "She's not the only one who..."
CTA: Read more stories / Shop what she bought
```

### TEACH
```
Subject: Behind-the-scenes reveal
Hook: Jade in her studio / factory floor / design process
Story: Origin of a specific design decision → materials → why it matters
Pivot: "Every detail is a choice"
CTA: See the collection
```

### PREPARE (day before sale)
```
Subject: Countdown/insider energy
Hook: Team scene — Alice printing lists, Julie pacing
Story: What's about to happen → what to expect → insider prep tips
Pivot: "Set your witch alarms"
CTA: Register / Bookmark / Check schedule
```

### CLOSE (last chance)
```
Subject: Direct urgency (no story needed)
Context Block: WHAT's ending, WHEN, specific scarcity numbers
Body: Short — 150-250 words. Facts + FOMO through specifics.
CTA: Shop now (multiple CTAs OK)
No story required. Pure urgency machine.
```

### LIVE (during sale)
```
Subject: Real-time stat or reaction
Context Block: Sale status update + current offers
Body: Timestamps, numbers, team chaos, customer reactions
CTA: Hunt link / specific product
Maximum energy. CAPSLOCK OK. Short paragraphs.
```

### THANK (post-campaign)
```
Subject: Celebration/gratitude
Hook: Results reveal — specific numbers
Story: What the community achieved together
Pivot: "This is what happens when witches show up"
CTA: Soft — community / next event tease
```

## 1C. Daily Email by Story Source

### Gothic Mundane
```
Hook: Specific mundane moment (time, place, coffee, grocery store)
Story: Ordinary situation → gothic twist → Julie's inner monologue
Turn: The moment where the mundane reveals something deeper
Pivot: Connect the feeling to a product that embodies it
Word count: 300-500
Characters: Julie (always), optional Alice/other
```

### Witch Victory (Customer Story)
```
Hook: Customer's exact words (quote_open is best hook type here)
Story: Julie reads the message → her reaction → flashback/connection
Turn: The moment Julie sees herself in the customer's story
Pivot: "She didn't just buy a bag. She..."
Word count: 350-500
Characters: Julie + The Witch (customer)
```

### Basic Brenda Encounter
```
Hook: Scene with Brenda (show actions, don't name her)
Story: Encounter → contrast → what it reveals about "normal" vs "us"
Turn: Julie's indifference IS the power move
Pivot: Product as armor / statement / identity
Word count: 300-450
Characters: Julie + Basic Brenda (shown, not named)
Rule: Max 1x/week. Mock archetype, not individuals.
```

### Product Origin
```
Hook: Jade in her studio OR specific design detail
Story: Why this product exists → design decisions → materials
Turn: The moment Jade (or Julie) realized this piece was special
Pivot: "That's why [product] feels different"
Word count: 300-450
Characters: Julie + Jade
```

### Cultural Darkness
```
Hook: Cultural moment (full moon, dark romance book, goth news)
Story: Julie's personal connection to the cultural moment
Turn: Why this resonates with "us" vs. the beige world
Pivot: Product as expression of that culture
Word count: 300-500
Characters: Julie (cultural moments are personal)
```

### Julie Personal
```
Hook: Specific personal moment (childhood, family, embarrassing)
Story: Vulnerability → revelation → strength
Turn: The realization that connects past to present
Pivot: What this means for the brand/product/community
Word count: 350-550 (longest type — personal stories need space)
Characters: Julie (+ family references)
```

### Industry Commentary
```
Hook: Industry news or trend that irritates Julie
Story: Julie's take → why mainstream fashion is broken → what we do differently
Turn: The hypocrisy or absurdity revealed
Pivot: "That's why Skullette..."
Word count: 300-450
Characters: Julie (confrontational mode)
```

# PART 2: SMS STRUCTURES

## By Pillar

### Pillar 1: Live Chaos
```
[timestamp] [real-time stat or reaction]
[1-2 lines of team chaos]
[CTA with link]
— Julie

Chars: 200-320 | Tone: Breathless, CAPSLOCK for peaks
When: During active sales
```

### Pillar 2: Insider Access
```
[Conspiratorial opener — "just between us"]
[What's coming/available early]
[Why you're getting this first]
[Link]
— Julie

Chars: 200-300 | Tone: Whispered, exclusive
When: Pre-launch, early access
```

### Pillar 3: Witch Check-in
```
[Casual question or observation]
[Personal touch — genuinely asking]
[Optional: small offer or reminder]
[Link if applicable]
— Julie

Chars: 100-200 | Tone: Friend texting
When: Between campaigns, HIGH segment only
```

### Pillar 4: The Dark Offer
```
[Product + price, upfront]
[One line of context/why]
[Scarcity if real]
[Link]
— Julie

Chars: 150-250 | Tone: Direct, no fluff
When: Flash deals, clearance
```

# PART 4: DEVELOPMENT GUIDE

## From SEED → Draft (8 steps)

1. **Pick a SEED** from Story Bank (or fresh idea)
2. **Identify the Story Source** (taxonomy Part 1B)
3. **Find the TURN** — what's the gap between expectation and reality?
4. **Place Julie IN the scene** — what is she doing when this happens?
5. **Add 2+ sensory details** — sight, sound, smell, texture, taste
6. **Draft the PIVOT** — how does the story naturally lead to the product?
7. **Write the HOOK** (often write this LAST — after you know the story)
8. **Draft P.S.** — thematically connected, single purpose

## When You're Stuck

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Can't find the hook | Write the middle first, then extract the hook |
| Story feels flat | Add a TURN — what goes wrong? What surprises Julie? |
| Pivot feels forced | The story should CREATE a need the product fills. If it doesn't, the story is wrong. |
| Too many ideas | Cut to ONE throughline. Save the rest as new SEEDs. |
| Voice feels off | Read 2-3 examples from `knowledge/brand/examples/` before rewriting |
