# Style Rules — Skullette
> Updated continuously from user feedback + post-mortems.
> **Read BEFORE writing any email or SMS.**

---

## Core Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Julie must be IN the story.** Her reaction = emotional anchor. Not narrating from outside.
2. **ONE story, ONE throughline per email.** Can't state it in one sentence? Too many messages.
3. **INHABIT scenes, don't summarize.** "Combat boots on wet pavement." > "She walked outside."
4. **Every scene needs a TURN.** Gap between expectation and reality. No turn = no scene.
5. **Offer in the FIRST THIRD of sale emails.** Never bury the deal. Cold reader must know what's being sold.
6. **Paste raw customer words.** Typos, "lol", emoji = proof of real person. Never paraphrase.
7. **SMS = texting her coven.** One thought per line. Fragments OK. No corporate speak. CAPSLOCK for chaos.
8. **Never apologize for darkness.** No validation-seeking. No "Please-Like-Me-itis." Indifference is power.
9. **Basic Brenda exists to polarize, not to hate.** Mock the archetype, not real women.
10. **No triple-And anaphora.** "And she... And she... And she..." = AI pattern. Cut or restructure.

## Extended Rules (From Kaizen Log + Feedback)

11. **Customer review emails need 2 layers.** Review story + personal connection. Not just "look at this great review."
12. **Polarization strongest when SHOWN.** "Didn't buy candles, didn't buy oatmeal cardigan" — show Brenda's actions, don't name the villain.
13. **Dual CTA works for gift-angle emails.** "Forward to your person" OR "Buy for yourself" → covers 2 segments in 1 email.
14. **Julie's vulnerability creates bond.** Childhood flashbacks, "I was that kid" — stronger than authority positioning.
15. **Fourth-wall breaks build intimacy.** "I need a minute" / "(Don't judge)" / parenthetical asides.
16. **Em dash (—) overuse = AI fingerprint.** Max 2-3 per email. Restructure if more.
17. **Opening = event/offer + hook in 3-5 lines.** Cold reader context first. No preamble.
18. **Product section stays in scene.** Show Julie with the product, not a listicle of features.
19. **P.S. jokes must be thematically connected.** Random P.S. = wasted real estate.
20. **No echo writing.** Don't repeat the same sentence structure 3x in a row.

## Teaser Email Checklist

- [ ] Sale-centric, not story-centric (hot dog = the offer, broccoli = the story)
- [ ] Each teaser = different angle (not repeating same message)
- [ ] Context block written with different tone/angle each time
- [ ] 100% standalone (cold reader understands without previous emails)
- [ ] ONE story, ONE throughline
- [ ] Julie is IN the story
- [ ] Scene has a TURN
- [ ] No pre-sell story ("If I told you..." = weak)
- [ ] Show, don't tell
- [ ] Paste raw customer words where applicable
- [ ] Recurring characters build across series
- [ ] Offer visible in first third

## Sale Email Checklist

- [ ] Offer in first 3-5 lines
- [ ] Specific scarcity (numbers, timestamps, not vague "hurry")
- [ ] Multiple CTAs (min 2 — product + collection)
- [ ] FOMO through scene, not through marketing language
- [ ] Unapologetic selling (no "sorry to bother you")
- [ ] Clear discount tiers if applicable

## SMS Checklist

- [ ] Under 320 chars core message
- [ ] One character max per SMS
- [ ] Lead with relationship, not deal
- [ ] No "VIP" or "EXCLUSIVE" labels
- [ ] End with warmth ("— Julie" or "Stay spooky")
- [ ] One joke/angle per SMS — don't stack
