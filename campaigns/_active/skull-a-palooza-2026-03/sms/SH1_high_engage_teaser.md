## CRITIQUE

### ✅ What's Working

| Element | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **"hey it's Julie."** | Perfect opener — casual, intimate, immediately personal. Feels like a real text, not marketing |
| **"ok I need to tell you something"** | Lean-in moment. Builds on SM1's curiosity without repeating it |
| **"I priced them myself and honestly I'm still not over it"** | Peak Julie — the (parenthetical honesty) baked into the sentence. Self-aware without being self-deprecating |
| **"I would genuinely be upset"** | Emotional stake. Not "don't miss out" — it's "I personally care if you miss this." Much stronger for High Engage |
| **"500+ witches already signed up"** | Social proof with tribe language. Clean |
| **"reply SKULL995E"** | Simple CTA. No friction |
| **Lowercase all the way** | Texting voice. Doesn't feel like a campaign |

### 🟡 Minor Refinements

| # | Issue | Suggestion | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"3-day private sale called SKULL-A-PALOOZA"** | Consider splitting: name drop first, then dates | Right now the name and dates land in one breath — the name deserves its own moment |
| 2 | **"leather bags and wallets from $9.95"** | Add a beat of disbelief or a specific bag call-out | The F13 SMS works because it names specific bags. Even one name ("like the Bloodrose") makes the price feel more insane |
| 3 | **"you're one of my favorite customers"** | Could lean harder into WHY — a specific behavior | "favorite customers" is warm but generic-adjacent. "You've been around since…" or "you always go for the weird ones" = more personal |
| 4 | **No parenthetical asides** | Add 1-2 signature (parenthetical thoughts) | Julie's voice leans heavily on parenthetical inner monologue. Zero feels slightly un-Julie |
| 5 | **"I'll send you the link march 17th at 10am"** | Good — but could tease what happens at 10am | Tiny missed opportunity to build anticipation for the moment |

## 🎪 DANIEL'S REVIEW

*[Adopting Daniel Throssell's voice — craft-focused, ridiculous, specific]*

---

Right. So. V2 is solid. It's clean, it's warm, it's Julie.

But here's the thing — it's **responsible** Julie. And responsible Julie is not the Julie who priced a $140 bag at $9.95 and then texted her best customers about it.

THAT Julie should sound like she's confessing to a crime she's not even slightly sorry about.

Three things I'd push:

**1. "Be A Little Mad" (the M in GAMESHOW)**

The price reveal is the moment. $9.95 for leather bags. That is *INSANE*. But V2 treats it like information. It should feel like Julie is having a small breakdown in real time. The reader should think "wow, she's actually lost it."

> V2: `leather bags and wallets starting at $9.95`
> `(I priced them myself and honestly I'm still not over it)`

Fine. But where's the MADNESS? Where's the moment she looks at what she's done and goes "...oh no"?

**2. Name a specific bag**

The F13 SMS works because it says "the BLOODROSE. the one that's normally $147." Not "leather bags." LEATHER BAGS is a category. The Bloodrose is a CHARACTER you're about to steal for an illegal price. Name one bag. Make it hurt.

**3. The closing tease needs a ridiculous image**

> V2: `you're gonna lose your mind when you see the prices`

That's a promise. Promises are boring. You know what's not boring? A specific, absurd, slightly unhinged image that makes them laugh AND want to see the prices.

## V2 vs V3 — Key Changes

| Element | V2 | V3 | Daniel's Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Opener** | "ok I need to tell you something" | "...and I need you to not tell anyone" | Conspiracy energy. She's letting you in on a SECRET. High Engage should feel like co-conspirators |
| **Price reveal** | 1 line + parenthetical | 3 lines: bag name → full price → breakdown | Name a specific bag (Morthwing, $140). Then spell out $9.95 in words. Forces the brain to process the insanity slowly |
| **Reaction moment** | "(I priced them myself and honestly I'm still not over it)" | Alice walks in, "Julie are you okay" / "no" | **Show don't tell.** A tiny SCENE instead of a statement. Daniel's Scene Construction principle. Also brings in team character (ref: F13 SMS #4) |
| **Closing** | "you're gonna lose your mind when you see the prices" | "you might need to sit down. I did. I'm still sitting." | Specific image > generic promise. "Still sitting" = she hasn't recovered. The madness is ONGOING |
| **Overall energy** | Warm, clean, professional Julie | Julie confessing a crime she's not sorry about | The "M" in GAMESHOW — Be A Little Mad. If the seller seems crazy, the deal seems crazier |
