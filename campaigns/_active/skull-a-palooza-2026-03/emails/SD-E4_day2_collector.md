## Email Body

Something happened at 2 PM today that I need to tell you about.

It was a normal afternoon. Jamie was packing orders with his earbuds in. Alice — our operations manager, the woman who rates entire sale days as "adequate" and considers that a compliment — was at her desk doing inventory counts.

Then Jamie's screen pinged.

He looked at it. Went quiet. Pulled out one earbud. Then the other.

Then he slowly — and I mean slowly, like he was handling evidence in a crime show — turned his monitor around so Alice could see it.

Twenty-seven items.

One order.

(I promised my accountant I wouldn't type the total. Let's just say it started with a 5.)

The warehouse went silent. Jamie's playlist was still going — some song about being unstoppable, which felt appropriate — but nobody was listening.

Alice stared at the screen.

"...Twenty-seven?"

"Twenty-seven."

Her face went through three phases.

Phase one: shock. Her eyebrows actually moved, which is rare.

Phase two: respect. She tilted her head slightly, the way she does when she encounters what she calls "competent behavior."

Phase three: she smiled.

With teeth.

I have worked with Alice for four years. I have seen her smile maybe six times. Twice at spreadsheets. Once at a perfectly organized supply closet. And now this.

This woman — this record-setting, twenty-seven-item, absolute legend of a witch — she basically adopted an entire section of our collection. She didn't browse our sale. She swept through it like she had a shopping list written in blood.

**→ [See the collection she swept through](https://skullette.com/skull-a-palooza)**

So. That happened.

But here's yours.

She's not alone. Today's orders have been bigger than Day 1. People aren't grabbing one item and leaving — they're building sets. Matching wallet, matching mini wallet, matching makeup bag. Some of you are walking out with three, four, five pieces.

And the collection is feeling it.

The handbags at $9.95? Gone. Don't even look for them. The Frostmourne and Deathmark makeup bags are hanging on by a thread. Mini wallets at $9.95 — still here, but thinning by the hour.

We've passed 400 orders. Less than 200 spots left in the entire sale. Tomorrow is the last day — and whatever's gone tonight stays gone.
