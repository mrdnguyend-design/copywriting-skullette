I got an email yesterday that made me feel like the least prepared person in my own company.

Jennifer wrote in and asked:

*"Do you have the small Lunacat handbag stocked for the Palooza event, or will it remain sold out?"*

She didn't ask what the deals are.

She didn't ask when the link drops.

She already knows ALL of that.

This woman has a specific bag picked out. A sold-out bag. And she's checking if we restocked it SPECIFICALLY for tomorrow.

Jennifer has a plan.

Do you?

Because I'll be honest. I don't think most of you do. And I know this because I asked my team if THEY had a plan, and what followed was the most ridiculous 30 minutes of my professional life.

Alice — you know Alice — she pulled out a printed spreadsheet.

Printed. On paper. Color-coded.

"Phase 1: Order processing. Phase 2: Quality check. Phase 3: Packing queue. Phase 4—"

I stopped her at Phase 4 because she had SEVEN PHASES and I was losing the will to live.

ME: "Alice. We sell bags. This isn't a shuttle launch."

ALICE: "Julie, last time we had a flash sale, the label printer jammed at order 47 and we lost 11 minutes. ELEVEN MINUTES. That doesn't happen again."

She pointed at the spreadsheet.

"Phase 5 is specifically labeled 'Printer Contingency.'"

(I run a leather bag company and my production head has a contingency plan for a PRINTER JAM. This is either the most prepared team in ecommerce or the most unhinged. Possibly both.)

Then Jamie walked in.

"Are we doing the thing where we talk about tomorrow? Because I'm ready."

Alice: "What's your plan, Jamie?"

He held up his phone. Playing his playlist. At full volume.

"Playlist is loaded. Four hours. Packing station is set. Coffee maker is prepped for 6am. And I'm going to bed at 7 tonight."

This was said at 4 in the afternoon.

Alice: "It's 4pm."

Jamie: "I know. I'm going to bed in three hours."

Alice: "WHY."

Jamie: "Peak packing form, Alice. You think I can pack at OLYMPIC SPEED on six hours of sleep? I need a FULL NINE."

Alice looked at me. Then back at Jamie. Then back at me.

"Julie. Please tell Jamie that going to bed at 7pm does not make him an athlete."

Jamie, already walking toward the door:

"I AM A PACKING ATHLETE, ALICE."

He turned around in the doorway.

"And tomorrow morning? When orders are flooding in and I'm packing at speeds you've never SEEN?"

He pointed at her spreadsheet.

"Your Phase 3 won't be able to keep up with me."

Then he left. We heard the playlist fade down the hallway.

Alice stared at his empty chair for a good five seconds.

Then she quietly wrote something on her spreadsheet.

I leaned over.

She'd added a new row to Phase 3:

*"Buffer time: Jamie."*
