## Email Body

I'm writing this email standing up because I am too wired to sit down.

We are at 545 orders. Fifty-five spots left. And for the last two hours, I've been watching something happen on Alice's screen that I need to tell you about.

Alice has a spreadsheet.

(Of course she does. It's Alice. She has a spreadsheet for her spreadsheets.)

It's the master inventory tracker. Every product, every design, every unit — all in her perfectly organized columns. And next to each row, there's a column she added this morning that simply says: **STATUS**.

When a design sells out, Alice highlights the row in red. Types "CLOSED" in the status column. And moves on to the next row.

No ceremony. No announcement. Just... red. Closed. Next.

Jamie has been watching this from the packing table. At some point this afternoon, he leaned over and whispered: "That spreadsheet needs a name."

Alice didn't look up. "It has a name. It's called Inventory Tracker Q1-2026."

Jamie said: "I'm calling it The Book of the Dead."

Alice paused. Looked at him. Looked back at her screen. And said absolutely nothing — which is how you know she approved.

The Book of the Dead. That's what we're working with now.

Here's today's death toll:

**The Bloodraven Mini Satchel** (March 19, 10:00 AM – 11:47 AM). Survived by 0 units. Cause of death: you witches. Alice highlighted. Typed. Moved on. Jamie whispered "rest in peace" from the packing table. Alice pretended not to hear him. (She heard him.)

**The Mournaire Circle Bag.** Over 50 snatched by noon. The last ones went around 3 PM. Alice highlighted. Typed. Jamie said "another one bites the dust" and Alice said "please stop narrating my spreadsheet." (She didn't actually want him to stop. I could tell.)

**The Bloodraven Circle Bag.** Gone at 3:45. Jamie pulled out his phone, found a trumpet playing Taps, and held it up toward Alice's monitor. Alice looked up, made eye contact, and said:

"One more song and I'm confiscating your phone."

Jamie put the phone down. But he did a tiny salute toward the screen. Alice saw it. And — I'm not making this up — the corner of her mouth twitched.

That's smile number eight this week. She's losing her edge. I'm concerned.

Meanwhile, Jamie has taped a piece of paper to the wall behind his packing station. It says "FALLEN HEROES" in Sharpie. He writes each sold-out design name on it when Alice makes the call. She told him to take it down. He didn't. It has 12 names on it. There's room for more.
